Calculate Your Body Mass Index

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight = <18.5 - You may need to increase your calorie intake to reach a healthier weight.
  • Normal weight = 18.5–24.9 - Congratulations! Your weight is within the healthy range.
  • Overweight = 25–29.9 - You may need to consider losing weight to reduce your risk of health problems.
  • Obesity = BMI of 30 or greater - You are at increased risk of developing serious health conditions. Consult with a healthcare professional for advice.

What Next? Take Action Towards Better Health:

  • Maintain a Healthy Weight: Maintaining a healthy weight is important for your heart health.
  • Increase Physical Activity: Moving more can lower your risk factors for heart disease.
  • Eat a Heart-Healthy Diet: Eating a healthy diet is the key to heart disease prevention.
  • Know and Control Your Heart Health Numbers: Tracking your heart health stats can help you meet your heart health goals.

Personalized Fitness Plan:

Based on your BMI category, here are some general exercise recommendations:

  • Underweight: Incorporate strength training exercises to build muscle mass.
  • Normal weight: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity per week.
  • Overweight: Focus on cardiovascular exercises such as walking, cycling, or swimming to burn calories and improve heart health.
  • Obesity: Start with low-impact exercises like swimming or cycling, and gradually increase intensity as fitness improves.

Meal Planning:

Here's a sample meal plan for your BMI category:

  • Breakfast: Whole grain toast with avocado and eggs.
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with mixed greens and vegetables.
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with quinoa and roasted vegetables.
  • Snacks: Greek yogurt with berries, nuts, or hummus with carrot sticks.
